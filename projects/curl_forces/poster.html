Statistical Mechanics of Hamiltonian Curl-Force Systems

Omer Chor 1 Aljaž Godec 2 Oren Raz 1
1 Department of Physics of Complex Systems, Weizmann Institute of Science, Rehovot, Israel
2 University of Freiburg, Germany

Motivation

Equilibrium systems coupled to a single heat bath always relax to the Boltzmann distribution $P \propto e^{-H/k_BT}$, fully determined by $H$ and $T$. No such universal principle exists for non-equilibrium steady states (NESS).

Certain two-dimensional systems admit a Hamiltonian description even when the force field is non-conservative. We ask:

Can the NESS of a Hamiltonian curl-force system be described by its Boltzmann distribution?

Answer: No — but the Hamiltonian structure reveals a clean mapping to a different class of nonequilibrium system.

Hamiltonian Curl-Forces

The unique Hamiltonian yielding a velocity-independent non-conservative force [1] is:

$$H(\mathbf{p},\mathbf{x}) = \frac{1}{2m}\mathbf{p}^T \mathcal{A}\, \mathbf{p} + U(\mathbf{x})$$

where $\mathcal{A}$ is a symmetric, position-independent matrix.

In 2D, WLOG $\mathcal{A}=\mathrm{diag}(\alpha,\gamma)$. The resulting force is $\mathbf{F} = -\mathcal{A}\nabla U$, with curl

$$\nabla\times\mathbf{F} = (\alpha-\gamma)\,\partial_{x_1}\partial_{x_2}U$$

Non-zero whenever $\alpha\neq\gamma$ and $U$ is not additively separable — i.e. the system is genuinely non-conservative.

KLEIN–KRAMERS EQ. $$\frac{\partial f}{\partial t} = \frac{(\mathcal{A}\nabla_{\mathbf{x}} U)^T\!\cdot\nabla_{\dot{\mathbf{x}}}f}{m} -\nabla_{\mathbf{x}} f\cdot\dot{\mathbf{x}} + \frac{\xi}{m}\nabla_{\dot{\mathbf{x}}}\cdot\!\left(\dot{\mathbf{x}}f + \frac{k_BT}{m}\nabla_{\dot{\mathbf{x}}}f\right)$$

Why Boltzmann Fails

Standard bath coupling gives equations of motion $$m\ddot{\mathbf{x}} = -\mathcal{A}\nabla U_\text{sys} - \nabla U_\text{int}$$ The friction and noise act on the physical velocity $\dot{\mathbf{x}}$, not on the canonical momentum $\mathbf{p}$.

Consequently, $H_\text{tot} = H_\text{sys}+H_\text{bath}+U_\text{int}$ is not conserved: the system cannot relax to the Boltzmann distribution $P\propto e^{-H/k_BT}$.

Substitution of $e^{-H/k_BT}$ into the Klein-Kramers equation yields a non-zero time derivative — confirmed directly by inspection.

Main Result: Canonical Transformation

Generating function $G_2 = \mathbf{P}^T\mathcal{A}^{-1/2}\mathbf{x}$ defines new coordinates and momenta:

$$\mathbf{X} = \mathcal{A}^{-1/2}\mathbf{x},\qquad \mathbf{P} = \mathcal{A}^{+1/2}\mathbf{p}$$

The transformed Hamiltonian $\tilde{H}=\frac{\mathbf{P}^2}{2m}+\tilde{U}(\mathbf{X})$ has isotropic kinetic energy — no curl-forces. The non-equilibrium nature reappears in the noise: each axis $i$ is effectively coupled to a bath at a different temperature,

$$T_i \equiv \mathcal{A}_{ii}^{-1}\,T$$

This establishes an exact duality between Hamiltonian curl-force systems and multi-temperature gyrators.

THE DUALITY $$\underbrace{\text{single bath } T,\ \mathcal{A}\neq\mathbf{1},\ \mathbf{F}=-\mathcal{A}\nabla U}_{\text{Hamiltonian curl-force system}} \;\;\stackrel{G_2}{\Leftrightarrow}\;\; \underbrace{\text{multiple baths } T_i=\mathcal{A}_{ii}^{-1}T, \ \tilde{\mathbf{F}}=-\nabla\tilde{U}}_{\text{multi-temperature gyrator}}$$

Application: Brownian Gyrator

Harmonic potential $$\tilde{U}(X,Y)=\frac{1}{2}(kX^2+2uXY+kY^2)$$ with baths $T_X\neq T_Y$ [2]. Applying the inverse transformation gives a curl-force system with constant curl:

$$\nabla\times\mathbf{F} = u\!\left(\frac{T_Y-T_X}{\sqrt{T_XT_Y}}\right)$$
  • Sign of curl predicts the direction of gyration without solving the Fokker–Planck equation.
  • Work is always applied to the curl-force system (single bath); heat flows between baths in the dual picture.
  • The entropy production rate is identical in both representations: $$\langle\dot{S}\rangle = \frac{k_B\xi u^2(T_Y-T_X)^2}{2T_XT_Y(\xi^2 k+u^2 m)}$$
EPR INVARIANCE $$\langle\dot{S}\rangle_{\text{curl-force}} \;=\; \langle\dot{S}\rangle_{\text{gyrator}} $$

Application: Gyrator on a Ring

Confining potential $$\tilde{U}(R)=\frac{1}{2}k(R-R_0)^2$$ with two orthogonal baths [3]. The dual curl-force field is radial: $$\mathbf{F}(\mathbf{x}) = -k\!\left(1 - \frac{\sqrt{T}\,R_0}{\sqrt{T_Xx^2+T_Yy^2}}\right)\mathbf{x}$$

The curl has a quadrupolar structure:

$$(\nabla\times\mathbf{F})\cdot\hat{z} = \frac{R_0 k\sqrt{T}(T_Y-T_X)\,xy}{(T_Xx^2+T_Yy^2)^{3/2}}$$

Summary & Outlook

  • Hamiltonian curl-force systems do not relax to the corresponding Boltzmann distribution when coupled to a heat bath.
  • A canonical transformation maps them exactly to multi-temperature systems. The curl $\nabla\times\mathbf{F}$ in the dual picture predicts the direction and structure of rotational probability currents.
  • The EPR is the same in both representations; heat and work rates differ — the two descriptions are physically distinguishable.
  • Experimental promise: curl-forces can be generated via optical tweezers, offering a route to experimentally simulate multi-temperature dynamics in standard laboratory settings.
  • Open questions: kinetic theory for curl force baths, overdamped regime, more general Hamiltonians, connection to information engines, unified nonequilibrium theory.
